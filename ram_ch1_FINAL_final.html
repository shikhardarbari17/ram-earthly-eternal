Chapter 1 - Page 1

Ayodhya, the Golden City

Long, long ago, in a beautiful city called Ayodhya, something very special happened.

A baby was born who would grow up to be one of the greatest people who ever lived. His name was Ram.

Ayodhya was full of golden palaces, blooming flowers, and the gentle sound of the river Saryu. The people of Ayodhya loved Ram more than anything in the world.

Chapter 1 - Page 2

Stories That Come Alive!

But why did everyone love Ram so much? It was not just because he was a prince.

People loved Ram because of how he lived. He always told the truth, even when it was hard. He kept his promises, even when it cost him everything.

He was kind to animals, birds, the poor, and the lonely. Grandmothers told his stories at bedtime. Farmers sang his name at sunrise. Children drew his picture in the dust.

Chapter 1 - Page 3

The Heart Never Forgets

Many, many years passed. The world changed. Buildings changed. Kings came and went.

But something never changed. Deep in their hearts, people never stopped believing in Ram.

Even when they could not visit his special place in Ayodhya, they prayed at home, sang his name, and kept the flame of faith alive - quietly, patiently, with great love.

"Because faith is not a building.
Faith is what lives inside you."
✦ Did You Know?

In India, people say "Ram Ram" as a greeting - just like saying "hello"! Even today, millions start their morning with Ram's name on their lips. 🙏

Chapter 1 - Page 4

A Prayer Answered! 🙏

After many, many years of waiting, hoping, and praying... something beautiful happened.

A magnificent temple began to rise in Ayodhya - made of stone and love and the prayers of millions of people.

People danced. People sang. People cried with happiness. It felt like the answer to a very, very long prayer. 🪔

🛕
Ram Temple rising - golden spires, diyas lit everywhere, people celebrating
✦ Video Scene - Higgsfield
✦ Amazing Fact!

On January 22nd, 2024, the Ram Temple in Ayodhya was consecrated. The whole of India lit diyas - it was bigger than Diwali for millions of people!
